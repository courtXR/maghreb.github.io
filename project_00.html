AR Portal

Client

Red Bull

Roles

Programmer
Product Designer
Interaction Designer

Date

Fall 2019

CONTEXT

AR Portal was the result of a collaborative project with Red Bull for my Design for Community Change class. The Brief asked us to help Red Bull drive app installs and increase user retention rates.


PROBLEM

Red Bull struggled to retain users since the mobile app offered very little unique content not found on its Youtube channel.


SOLUTION

LoL AR Portal is a feature exclusive to the Red Bull TV app that would allow esports fans to watch League of Legends pro matches using augmented reality. This feature provides a unique experience to Red Bull’s fastest-growing demographic, esports fans. This feature will allow Red Bull to capture millions of esports fans worldwide as new users and strengthen their relationship with their largest global partner, Riot Games.

User Interviews

Interviewing users about the RBTV app revealed that most users didn’t see any difference between the app and others like Youtube when it came to video content. The only features users thought was interesting were the 360 video and AR features but felt that the content wasn’t suitable to the format. Both the AR and 360 video features turned out to be convoluted methods to navigate to or view flat video content. This realization disappointed each user I interviewed.

Competitor Analysis

During a competitor analysis, I noticed that Red Bull was unique in the fact that they were a video streaming platform that had experience with AR. Some companies supported 360 video content and even VR but when it came to AR, Red Bull was the outlier.

Secondary Research

Through research, I found that Red Bull had long been trying to break into the esports market. Although they’ve gained respect among fans through the years, only recently had they made their largest move yet. In 2019 Red Bull was named the official drink of League of Legends, the largest esport in the world, and a Riot games global partner.

Research Insights

I saw an opportunity for Red Bull to appeal to its growing esports demographic and appeal to its largest partner, Riot Games, by using a feature entirely unique to their app, AR. Companies like Valve have begun streaming esports like DOTA 2 using their Valve Index VR headset. However, VR remains intimidating to the general public due to its high cost and the threat of VR sickness. AR is a technology that Red Bull has significant experience with and is already popular with the general public due to easy access and ease of use of popular apps like Pokémon Go.

Sketches

I decided early on that using AR to allow fans to pan and zoom around the summoners rift arena would be the core experience of this app feature.

Prototype

Early prototypes show the summoners rift arena and a character model from the game to give users the impression that they are watching live gameplay. Hi-fidelity prototypes were built and coded using the Unity game engine.

Testing

For testing, users were given a working app they could try the feature on. They were asked a series of questions to measure engagement and usability. In later iterations, a live video feed of gameplay was added to provide context to what users were seeing happening on the field.

Reflection

From the testing, I found that esports fans and non-esports fans found this feature to be a definite selling point of the app. Users who had never watched a LoL match stated that this feature would certainly encourage them to download the app and share it with friends and family.
