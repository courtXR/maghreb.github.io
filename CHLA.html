The Brief

CHLA approached our program to help them solve the issue of connectivity between promotoras and their clients. CHLA only has two promotoras on staff helping on average 50 families a day. Tasks include distributing paperwork and scheduling appointments and assisting clients who may be illiterate.

SEDOH and Mental health for LatinX communities


Stakeholder Interviews

We were given the opportunity to speak with a promotora at CHLA. We learned that among the list of requested services at CHLA help in the area of mental health was the highest. We decided to make this a focus in terms of improving one area of the SEDOH scale.

Research

While researching Latinx family access to mental health services we discovered that it was largely uncommon for families to obtain these services. This was either due to a lack of access in their area, price, and lack of scheduling opportunities.
We decided to do some research on alternative forms of therapy and discovered the effectiveness of AI therapist.

First prototype

We turned this idea into a prototype that focused on an AI family therapist that uses natural language processing. We got feedback from stakeholders that this would not be effective since CHLA was only responsible for connecting clients with mental health services not administering the services themselves. So we decided to go in a different direction.

Navigating Complex Services for Immigrant Families


Research

Looking back at our interview with a promotora at CHLA we remembered her statement of how often she had to assist families who were not comfortable navigating complex forms and paperwork. We did some additional research on how many extra forms immigrant families were expected to fill out compared to other families. These laws were created by the government to deter immigrant families from accessing services. The government would use these extra stipulations to disqualify applicants for small infractions which was an additional fact we learn from the promotora we interviewed.

Second prototype

With this prototype, we designed a system to assist families in filling out forms with limited help from promotoras. The system would answer questions in through natural language, and provide tips and definitions for complicated terminology. We got very positive feedback from stakeholders with this prototype. They particularly enjoyed the way the app allowed people to ask questions and receive feedback.

Preventing lost of coverage


Research

Inspired by the research we found during research for our last prototype we decided our main focus should be on preventing loss of coverage by helping clients complete paperwork.

Third prototype

As a result, we created a feature that reminded clients and promotoras if they were in danger of losing coverage. We also made it easier for clients to contact promotoras if they needed help. We felt found a good balance between giving people the help they needed without completely replacing promotoras.

Conclusion

We designed this app to be a trustworthy and reliable way for families to get immediate help and contact promotoras if needed. We think this will relieve a lot of the workload for promotoras and families. With this app, families will be able to keep up to date with their status and remain in control of their healthcare coverage.
